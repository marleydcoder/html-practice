Here is the timeline of nelson mandea's life

  • Rolihlahla Mandela was born on 18 July 1918.
  • He attended primary school in Qunu where he was called the name Nelson, in accordance with the custom of giving all school children Christian names.
  • He completed his Junior Certificate at Clarkebury Boarding Institute and went on to Healdtown, a Wesleyan secondary school of some repute, where he matriculated.
  • Mandela began his studies for a Bachelor of Arts degree at the University College of Fort Hare but did not complete the degree there as he was expelled for joining in a student protest.
  • Fled to johannesburg and later completed his BA through the University of South Africa and went back to Fort Hare for his graduation in 1943.
  • Mandela, while increasingly politically involved from 1942, only joined the African National Congress in 1944 when he helped to form the ANC Youth League (ANCYL).
  • In 1944 he married Evelyn Mase, a nurse.They had two sons, Madiba Thembekile "Thembi" and Makgatho, and two daughters both called Makaziwe, the first of whom died in infancy. He and his wife divorced in 1958.
  • Mandela was arrested in a countrywide police swoop on 5 December 1956, which led to the 1956 Treason Trial.
  • During the trial Mandela married a social worker, Winnie Madikizela, on 14 June 1958. They had two daughters, Zenani and Zindziswa. The couple divorced in 1996.
  • mandela secretly left south africa, he travelled around africa and visited england to gain support.
  • He received military training in Morocco and Ethiopia and returned to South Africa in July 1962.
  • He was arrested on august 5,charged and convicted to 5 years in prison where he started serving in pritoria.
  • On 9 October 1963 Mandela joined 10 others on trial for sabotage in what became known as the Rivonia Trial. While facing the death penalty his words to the court at the end of his famous "Speech from the Dock" on 20 April 1964 became immortalised:
    • "I have fought against white domination, and I have fought against black domination. I have cherished the ideal of a democratic and free society in which all persons live together in harmony and with equal opportunities. It is an ideal which I hope to live for and to achieve. But if needs be, it is an ideal for which I am prepared to die."
  • On 11 June 1964 Mandela and seven other accused,were convicted and the next day were sentenced to life imprisonment.
